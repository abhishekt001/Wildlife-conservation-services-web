• Project Asian Elephant
    India is the home to largest populations of wild Asian elephants which are mainly concentrated in the Western ghats. An IUCN report states that there has been approximately 50% decline in its population in the last 75 years.   • Indian Wildlife Preservers
    Wild Buffalo Scheme
    The wild buffalo is listed in Schedule I of the Indian WildLife (Protection) Act, 1972. IUCN declared Wild Buffalo endangered as less than 4000 individuals are estimated to be remaining in the wild. The scheme aims to stabilise the wild buffalo population in Udanti-Sitanadi Tiger Reserve, Chhattisgarh by implementing ex situ and in situ interventions. With help of Chhattisgarh Forest Department, formulated a five-year Action Plan for the recovery of the wild buffalo population in Udanti region.
  • Indian Wildlife Preservers
    Project GIB
    TThe Great Indian Bustard or GIB is a unique species of bird declared as ‘Critically Endangered’ by the IUCN. We’ve successfully developed monitoring networks in some districts of Rajasthan to gather crucial information on the Great Indian Bustard. Constant monitoring, periodic health examinations and treatment as when required are being undertaken
  • Indian Wildlife Preservers
    Prohibit Panda Poaching/PPP
    With a population of less than 10,000 and constantly declining, Red Pandas have been listed as endangered under the Red List. One of the most concerning reasons behind this is poaching and trading of Red panda’s body parts. For decades, we've been educating people about the support to the Forest Department in the prevention of wildlife trafficking
